The Honest Numbers — Today

How the season is built from real days, real revenue, and where the gap is.

Weekend vs. midweek

Right now, roughly $526,000 of a typical season comes from Friday through Sunday — about 76% of the season from three nights. The bar is open Wednesday through Sunday; only Monday and Tuesday does the dock sit empty (you're closed), so those two days earn $0 in the baseline. Wed through Sun the dock is in use — Wed & Thu still have room to grow on top of traffic you already get.

Overall current baseline

The full-season total used in the proposal is $693,000 (revenue while open Wed–Sun only; Mon & Tue closed — same weekly shape as before, scaled to reflect a slower April and a quiet October). That number is the anchor for every “growth” and “multiplier” calculation in the rest of the deck — not a guess, but a line in the sand.

What “+$28K/week” means

Day-level targets add up to a $28,000/week lift — $672,000 over 24 weeks — including opening Monday and Tuesday (currently closed, modeled at $2K/night each) and growing Wed–Sun. That’s before layering on the overall revenue multiplier from marketing and events. Merch is not included in these bar numbers.

How we’ll track it

Weekly revenue totals (by day where possible) let us compare this season to last, week over week. The proposal is built so that you keep 100% of the baseline — anything on growth above that line is negotiated in person.
