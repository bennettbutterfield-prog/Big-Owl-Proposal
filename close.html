The Day We Sign & the First 90 Days

How we use the two weeks before opening — and what “success” looks like at 30, 60, and 90 days.

Day one (deal signed)

Social profiles get a coordinated refresh; ad accounts and pixels are verified; event packages and rate sheets go into draft; first influencer outreach lists go out; the content calendar for opening week is locked.

Week 1–2 → opening

Opening-day shoot, first paid campaigns live, first short-form posts scheduled, “first boats” style promos in boating groups, and a clear story for staff so the guest experience matches the marketing.

30 days

Baseline reporting is clean; we’re comparing week-over-week; first event inquiries are in the pipeline; creative is iterating on what’s actually performing.

60 days

Signature experiences are repeating; lifts on Wed & Thu and on new Mon & Tue nights show in the numbers; events are contributing to the story; we double down on what works.

90 days

Enough data to judge results honestly: revenue trend, event book, and cost per acquisition. If the trajectory isn’t there, the agreement’s exit window is how you protect yourself.
