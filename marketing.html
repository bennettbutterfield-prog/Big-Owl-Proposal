Facebook & Instagram ads (Meta)

Geo and demographic targeting as above — conversion-focused campaigns promoting nightly and weekly specials and upcoming musical acts. Creatives rotate with the calendar so ads always mirror what’s on the dock this week.

Conversion measurement: Engagement and ROAS are tied to real behavior — coupons redeemed at the bar (or equivalent tracked offers). That closes the loop: ad → click → visit → redemption → revenue we can attribute.

SEO

How it works: Search engines rank pages by relevance, authority, and usefulness. When someone types “dock bar near me,” “happy hour Kent Island,” or “things to do this weekend,” Google matches those queries to pages that clearly answer the intent. Why it matters: Organic traffic is durable — it compounds while you sleep — and it catches high-intent planners (tourists, boaters, locals) who never saw an ad.

How we implement: We’ll optimize The Big Owl’s website around keywords tied to real search behavior — e.g. dock bar, happy hour, things to do Kent Island, waterfront tiki, live music, and private events. A regular blog gives us fresh, indexable pages for events, artist spotlights, and seasonal content so you rank for long-tail searches competitors ignore. Technical basics (titles, headings, internal links, page speed, mobile) support everything else. Google Business Profile and Maps stay accurate too — hours, photos, reviews, Q&A — so local “near me” discovery stays sharp.

Weekly newsletter

A weekly email for members of the awards program — your enrolled, opted-in regulars. Each issue includes a fun recap of the week, who’s playing next (musical acts), and specials worth coming back for. The goal is to get program members out more often, deepen an engaged community, and create shareable moments they’ll forward to friends. Calendar stays aligned with social and ads.

TikTok & Reels

Search behavior: A huge share of “what should we do tonight?” now starts in TikTok and Reels — people search and scroll for local food, drink, and live music before they ever open Google. Short video is discovery and SEO for the algorithm.

Weekly content strategy: Consistent posts (hooks, events, behind-the-bar, sunset, acts) aimed at organic reach and search-style discovery (strong captions, on-screen text, and keywords people actually type). Most searches now start on social — so every post is shaped for how people look for things to do near Kent Island and the Shore.

Micro-influencers

Smaller accounts often deliver outsized engagement and traffic versus generic celebrity posts — their audiences trust them and actually live in the region.

Strategy: Invite a selected set of micro-influencers to influencer event days at Big Owl. They create content on-site in exchange for comped drinks (structured, on-brand, within your policies). The clips and photos they produce can be reused as ad creative in Meta campaigns — authentic, native-feeling ads that outperform polished studio shots.

Instagram feed

Instagram is the brand showroom — consistent look, stories for urgency, highlights for menus and music. Content aligns with what’s running on TikTok/Reels and in ads so the feed always matches the real week on the dock.

How we measure success

Platform metrics (reach, CTR, CPM) for learning; coupon redemptions and ROAS for conversion campaigns; covers, bar revenue, and event inquiries as the real scoreboard. Reporting lines up with the weekly numbers in the deck.
